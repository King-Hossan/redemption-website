Grade 8 Mathematics

1. Integers

Integers include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero. Operations on integers follow the rules of arithmetic but require special attention to signs.

Tips:

  • When adding integers with the same sign, keep the sign and add their absolute values.
  • When adding integers with different signs, subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger and keep the sign of the larger absolute value.
  • Multiplication and division follow these rules: same signs give a positive result; different signs give a negative result.

Examples

  • 1. -3 + (-5) = -8
  • 2. -4 - (-6) = -4 + 6 = 2
  • 3. -7 × 3 = -21
  • 4. -20 ÷ 5 = -4
  • 5. A debt of KSh 400 is reduced by a payment of KSh 250 → -400 + 250 = -150

Practice Questions

  • 1. A student loses 5 points and gains 8 points. What is the final score difference?
  • 2. The temperature drops from 10°C to -2°C. Find the change in temperature.
  • 3. A submarine is 150m below sea level. It ascends 60m. Where is it now?
  • 4. John owes KSh 300 and repays KSh 100. What is the remaining balance?
  • 5. A football team gains 3 points in a win and loses 2 points in a penalty. What is the net gain after both?
  • 6. -12 + 7 = ?
  • 7. -6 × (-4) = ?
  • 8. -28 ÷ (-7) = ?
  • 9. 5 - (-9) = ?
  • 10. A plane descends 1000 ft and then climbs 400 ft. What is its final position?

2. Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole. They can be proper, improper, or mixed numbers. Operations include addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Tips:

  • Find the Lowest Common Denominator (LCD) when adding or subtracting fractions.
  • Multiply fractions straight across.
  • To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor.

Examples

  • 1. 1/4 + 2/3 = (3 + 8)/12 = 11/12
  • 2. 5/6 - 1/3 = (5 - 2)/6 = 3/6 = 1/2
  • 3. 2/5 × 3/4 = 6/20 = 3/10
  • 4. 4/7 ÷ 2/3 = 4/7 × 3/2 = 12/14 = 6/7
  • 5. A recipe needs 2/3 cup of sugar and 3/4 cup of flour. Total ingredients = 2/3 + 3/4 = 17/12 = 1 5/12 cups

Practice Questions

  • 1. A farmer harvested 2/5 of his field on Monday and 1/3 on Tuesday. What fraction was harvested in total?
  • 2. A cake was cut into 8 slices. Jane ate 3/8, and John ate 2/8. How much of the cake was eaten?
  • 3. Simplify: 5/6 + 1/4
  • 4. Multiply: 3/7 × 2/3
  • 5. Divide: 4/5 ÷ 2/3
  • 6. Subtract: 7/10 - 3/5
  • 7. A jug contains 3/4 L of milk. 2/5 L is used. How much is left?
  • 8. If a tank is 2/3 full and 1/6 is drained, how much remains?
  • 9. Express 9/12 in simplest form.
  • 10. Add: 3 1/4 + 2 2/3

3. Decimals

Decimals are another way to represent fractions and parts of a whole using powers of ten. Operations include addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Tips:

  • Align decimal points when adding or subtracting.
  • Ignore decimal points while multiplying, then count total decimal places in the answer.
  • To divide decimals, shift the decimal in both divisor and dividend to make the divisor a whole number.

Examples

  • 1. 3.45 + 2.1 = 5.55
  • 2. 7.8 - 4.32 = 3.48
  • 3. 1.2 × 0.3 = 0.36
  • 4. 6.25 ÷ 2.5 = 2.5
  • 5. A bag weighs 3.5 kg and another weighs 2.75 kg. Total weight = 6.25 kg

Practice Questions

  • 1. Add: 4.2 + 3.35
  • 2. Subtract: 8.5 - 6.15
  • 3. Multiply: 0.6 × 0.9
  • 4. Divide: 7.2 ÷ 0.6
  • 5. A student scores 12.5, 14.25, and 13.75 marks. What is the total?
  • 6. Round 3.786 to two decimal places
  • 7. Express 3/4 as a decimal
  • 8. Convert 0.625 to a fraction
  • 9. Increase 4.5 by 25%
  • 10. A piece of wire is 5.75 m. 2.3 m is cut off. What length remains?
