1. Combined Operations on Integers

Combined operations involve the use of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division in a single expression involving integers. To solve such expressions, use the BODMAS rule: Brackets, Orders (powers/roots), Division and Multiplication (from left to right), Addition and Subtraction (from left to right).

Examples

  • 1. Simplify: -4 + 7 - 3 × 2 → -4 + 7 - 6 = -3
  • 2. Simplify: (-3) × (4 - 6) ÷ 2 → (-3) × (-2) ÷ 2 = 6 ÷ 2 = 3
  • 3. A football team gained 3 points in a win, lost 2 points in a loss, and had 5 wins and 2 losses. What is their total? → 5×3 - 2×2 = 15 - 4 = 11
  • 4. James earns KSh 50 for every correct answer and loses KSh 20 for each incorrect one. If he answers 8 correctly and 3 incorrectly, what is his score? → 50×8 - 20×3 = 400 - 60 = KSh 340
  • 5. Simplify: -12 ÷ 3 + 2 × (-4) = -4 + (-8) = -12

2. Indices and Logarithms

Indices are powers or exponents used to express repeated multiplication. For example, 23 = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.
Logarithms are the inverse of indices. If 23 = 8, then log2(8) = 3.

Laws of indices:

  • am × an = am+n
  • am ÷ an = am-n
  • (am)n = amn
  • a0 = 1 (if a ≠ 0)

Examples

  • 1. Simplify: 23 × 22 = 25 = 32
  • 2. Simplify: (52)3 = 56 = 15625
  • 3. A factory doubles its output each week. If it produces 100 units in week 1, how many units in week 4? → 100 × 23 = 800
  • 4. Peter invests KSh 1000 in a bank that doubles his money each year. How much after 3 years? → 1000 × 23 = KSh 8000
  • 5. Express 81 as a power of 3 → 81 = 34
